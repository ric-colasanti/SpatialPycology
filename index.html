    3. Inky (also known as the "Blue Ghost" or "Bashful"): Inky is the third ghost in the line and is the most unpredictable of the ghosts. He is programmed to try to ambush Pac-Man, but his behavior is influenced by the positions of both Pac-Man and Blinky.
    4. Clyde (also known as the "Orange Ghost" or "Pokey"): Clyde is the last ghost in the line and is programmed to chase Pac-Man, but will sometimes stop and return to his home base. He is the slowest of the ghosts and is the least aggressive.

The Separating Axis Theorem (SAT)

The Separating Axis Theorem (SAT) is a mathematical result that provides a way to determine if two convex polygons are colliding in two-dimensional space. The basic idea behind SAT is that if there exists a line (called an axis) that separates the two polygons, then they are not colliding. Conversely, if no such separating axis exists, then the polygons are colliding. To check for collisions using SAT, we first project each polygon onto a set of axes that are perpendicular to the edges of the polygons. We then check if there exists an axis along which the projections of the two polygons do not overlap. If such an axis exists, then the polygons are not colliding. If no such axis exists, then the polygons are colliding. In practice, we can optimize the SAT algorithm by only checking axes that are perpendicular to the edges of the polygons. This is because if the polygons are colliding, then there must exist at least one such axis along which the projections overlap. By checking only these axes, we can often quickly determine if the polygons are colliding or not. SAT is a powerful and efficient algorithm for collision detection between convex polygons, and it is widely used in computer graphics and physics engines.
